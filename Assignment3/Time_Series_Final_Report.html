4Ô∏è‚É£ Model Behavior and Interpretations

Each architecture exhibited distinct learning dynamics and predictive behavior throughout the training process. The LSTM effectively modeled long-term dependencies but required more epochs to converge, whereas the GRU provided faster training with slightly reduced accuracy. The CNN + LSTM emerged as the optimal hybrid model, integrating convolutional layers that captured localized temporal features (e.g., short-term fluctuations) before feeding them into recurrent layers that learned global sequential dependencies.

Key Insights:
  • Convergence Stability: The CNN + LSTM model showed smoother loss convergence and minimal overfitting signs.
  • Feature Hierarchy: CNN layers extracted fine-grained features, improving LSTM‚Äôs temporal reasoning.
  • Error Distribution: Residuals centered around zero indicate low bias and good generalization capability.
  • Robustness: The small gap between validation and test MAE proves that the hybrid model generalizes well to unseen data.

5Ô∏è‚É£ Visual and Analytical Interpretation

The CNN + LSTM architecture demonstrates an ideal balance between precision and interpretability. The convolutional layers act as pattern detectors for localized temporal shifts‚Äîdetecting short bursts, trends, or anomalies‚Äîwhile the LSTM layer establishes context and long-range relationships over the full sequence.

This combination allows the model to capture the complete spectrum of temporal relationships inherent in complex real-world time-series data.

üèÅ Final Conclusion

The comparative analysis concludes that the CNN + LSTM hybrid model significantly outperforms traditional recurrent networks for time-series forecasting. By leveraging convolutional layers for short-term feature extraction and LSTM units for long-term sequence learning, it achieved the lowest validation MAE and delivered more stable, interpretable, and accurate predictions.

Future enhancements should focus on integrating attention mechanisms and transformer architectures to capture even more intricate temporal dependencies and improve interpretability. Additionally, employing multivariate forecasting (including external predictors such as temperature, humidity, or seasonality indices) could further refine performance in real-world applications.
